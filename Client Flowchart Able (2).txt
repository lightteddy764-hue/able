Example of Triage Process:
1. AI Assistant Introduction:
* "Welcome to Able! I'm here to assist you on your journey to mental wellness. Let's start by getting to know you better."
2. Collect Basic Information:
* "What's your name?"
* "How old are you?"
* "How are you feeling today? Please describe your current mental state."
* "Do you have any specific concerns or issues you'd like to address?"
3. Administer WHODAS 2.0 Checklist:
* "Please answer the following questions about how much difficulty you had in the last 30 days due to health/mental health conditions."
4. WHODAS 2.0 Questions:
* Understanding and Communicating:
* "How much difficulty did you have concentrating on doing something for ten minutes?" (None, Mild, Moderate, Severe, Extreme or cannot do)
* "How much difficulty did you have remembering to do important things?" (None, Mild, Moderate, Severe, Extreme or cannot do)
* ... (Continue with all WHODAS 2.0 items)
5. Assess Severity Based on WHODAS 2.0 Scores:
* Calculate the total score and domain scores.
* Determine the level of disability: Higher scores indicate greater disability.
6. Referral for Serious Conditions:
* If the client indicates severe symptoms (e.g., severe depression, suicidal thoughts, psychosis):
* "It sounds like you might need immediate support from a mental health professional. Here are some links to trusted psychiatric services that can help you right away."
* Provide links to emergency services and psychiatrists.
7. Suggestions for Mild to Moderate Conditions:
* If the client indicates mild to moderate symptoms:
* "Thank you for sharing. Here are some services that might help you on your wellness journey:"
1. Enable-Healing Zone: "Connect with world-class licensed and verified therapists to help you in your wellness journey."
2. Capable-Body-Mind Connection: "Benefit from holistic health practices including food for mental wellness, yogic practices, breathing techniques, and routine management."
3. Suitable-Community Circle: "Join our community experiences such as the Mental Spa, Wellness Dating, and Wellness Meetups."
4. Viable-Youth Support: "Explore dedicated services for children and young adults, including aptitude testing, career advising, and wellness support for anxiety, body-image issues, bullying, and more."
5. Valuable-Voice Platform: "Share your story, experience, suggestions, and concerns with our supportive community."
